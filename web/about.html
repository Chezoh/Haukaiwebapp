Māori culture and kai

kai

/kʌɪ/ noun: informal/ meaning = food.

Traditionally Māori were hunters, gatherers and crop farmers, sourcing kai whenua (food from the land) and kai moana (seafood). Examples of the types of food eaten include kumara (sweet potato) and taewa tutaekuri (purple potato), puha - a wild growing leafy green vegetable, and watercress, rewena – a traditional sour dough style bread, birds – such as Mutton bird, and seafood such as fish, mussels, kina (sea eggs), scallops, freshwater crayfish and paua (abalone). Herbs and seasoning came from plants such as harakeke (flax) - used as an oil or seasoning, and horopito to add pepper style seasoning. These foods are used to form the basis of many of the dishes at Haukai restaurant.

What to expect when dining with us

We welcome everyone, from all background and experiences to dine with us at Haukai. Our team will run through the menu with you and answer any questions you may have about any of the food on offer, as we understand that not everyone knows what all the ingredients are! Once your food is brought to the table, we will guide you through a short karakia (prayer) to offer thanks and gratitude.

Karakia Mo te Kai (Grace)
Whakapainga ēnei kai (Bless our Food)
Hei oranga mo o matou tinana (As well-being for our body)
Ko Ihu Karaiti hoki tō mātou Ariki (For Jesus Christ our Lord)
Āmine (Amen)

About us

Alicia and Matiu Tahu opened Haukai restaurant in the Bay of Islands to create a restaurant for locals and visitors to the area where they could come and enjoy both Māori and Pakeha kai (food), as well as experience Te Ao Māori through the dining experience. Taking inspiration from the traditional kai prepared at home whilst growing up, Alicia went on to study cookery after finishing high school, and brings that experience combined with family tradition to create the menu at Haukai.
